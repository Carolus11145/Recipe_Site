Chicken Harees

What is Harees?

Arabian Harisa, also known as hareesha or jahrees, is a Ramandan specialty primarily made with wheat and barley. It is a delicacy of the Arabian Peninsula and one of the Middle East's finest meaty meals.

Ingredients you will need

  • Chicken - 1/2 Kg (Boneless)
  • Wheat - 1/2 Kg (soaked overnight and drained)
  • Barley - 1/2 Kg (soaked overnight and drained)
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste - 1 tbsp
  • Green Chili Paste - 2 tbsp
  • Cumin Seeds - 1/2 tbsp (Crushed)
  • Fennel Seeds - 1 tbsp (Crushed)
  • Salt
  • Chat Masala - 1 tbsp
  • Lemon Juice - 2 tbsp
  • Edible Oil - for tempering
  • Olive Oil - 1 tbsp
  • Haleem Masala - 1/2 tbsp
  • Brown Fried Onions
  • Coriander Leaves - 1 tbsp

Steps:

  1. Boil the soaked barley and wheat in a pan with double water quantity. If you choose to use a pressure cooker it will take around half an hour. Otherwise, it will take roughly an hour.
  2. Churn the wheat and barley with a blender until it gains a paste-like consistency. Add a little water if you feel it is too thick.
  3. Mix together the ginger-garlic paste, salt and crushed cumin and fennel seeds in a seperate pan. Shred the chicken and put it to one side.
  4. Add the shredded chicken and the paste to the wheat-barley mixture along with the chicken stock. Mix well and allow it to cook for another 15 minutes. You can add chat masala and lemon juice.
  5. Here you can opt to temper with only oil or with Haleem masala. Top the Harees with haleem masala, then heat oil in a pan and pour it over the Harees. Close the lid so the smell is absorbed.
  6. The dish is officially ready to serve. Garnish with fried onions, coriander and a table spoon of ghee.
